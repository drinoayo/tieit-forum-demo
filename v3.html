What is the best approach for Letter of Credit discrepancies in West Africa?
CA
Caleb Afuwape· Student · 3 hours ago · 12 replies
CA
Caleb Afuwape
Student · 3 hours ago · Original post
I recently encountered a situation where our LC documents were rejected by the confirming bank due to a discrepancy in the description of goods. The shipper's invoice says "fresh cassava starch" but the LC specifies "cassava starch, food grade." The goods are the same — we have the certification to prove it. What is the standard practice in West Africa for resolving this kind of documentary discrepancy?
BA
Bamidele Ayemibo
Verified Mentor · Trade Finance Director · 1 hour ago
Best answer
Great question Caleb. This is a classic UCP 600 Article 14 scenario. Under Article 14(d), the description of goods in a commercial invoice must correspond with the LC, but in other documents it need not mirror the LC exactly.

Your approach should be:

1. Request an amendment from the issuing bank to align the description. Most cooperative banks process this within 24-48 hours if your buyer agrees.

2. Alternatively, the presenting bank can note the discrepancy and present under reserve — payment proceeds but the buyer retains the right to reject. This carries risk.

In West Africa specifically, calling the trade desk directly tends to speed resolution considerably.
