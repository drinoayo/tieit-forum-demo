What is the best approach for Letter of Credit discrepancies in West Africa?

AO
Adaeze Okonkwo · Student · Posted 3 hours ago · 12 replies
AO

Adaeze Okonkwo

Student · 3 hours ago · Original post

I recently encountered a situation where our LC documents were rejected by the confirming bank due to a discrepancy in the description of goods. The shipper's invoice says "fresh cassava starch" but the LC specifies "cassava starch, food grade." The goods are the same — we have the certification to prove it. What is the standard practice in West Africa for resolving this kind of documentary discrepancy, and how quickly can it typically be resolved before the shipment deadline?
CA

Caleb Afuwape

Verified Mentor · Head of Trade Finance · 1 hour ago

Best answer
Great question, Adaeze. This is a classic UCP 600 Article 14 scenario. The key issue is that under Article 14(d), the description of goods in a commercial invoice must correspond with the LC, but in other documents it need not mirror the LC exactly — it just must not conflict. Your approach here should be:

1. Request an amendment from the issuing bank to align the description — this is standard and most cooperative banks will process it within 24–48 hours if your buyer agrees.

2. Alternatively, the presenting bank can note the discrepancy and present under reserve, meaning payment proceeds but the buyer retains the right to reject. This is riskier.

In West Africa specifically, calling the trade desk directly (not email) tends to speed things up considerably. Who is your confirming bank?
